Behavioural Initial Assessment

What it's all about

Some issues require a trained and experienced behaviourist and lucky for you, both Rachel and Laura are just that! In a behavioural assessment, we will visit you and your dog in your home to see them in their environment, have an in-depth talk about their lifestyle, health, social interactions, history and so on. The session will last for up to 2 hours, giving you and your dog the time and support you need for us to gain the best understanding of the issues you're having. We will also go out 'on location' to assess the issue if it's appropriate for the challenges your dog is having. A behavioural assessment is needed for issues such as dog-dog reactivity, human directed aggression, phobias and anxieties, aversion to handling and triggers etc.

We believe that changing any behavioural issues requires a holistic approach, which means that you'll also receive a full written assessment of the session and our insight into all aspects of your dogs life that will positively impact and compliment the training going forward. Your mentor will create a personalised management plan for you to start as soon as you can along with training tutorials and support. This option also includes a weekly check in call with your mentor to give you the support you and your dog need in-between sessions.

"Does my dog need behavioural or training help?"

It can be a little difficult to know whether your dog needs behavioural or training help so here is a list of some of the differences. There are many more that could be added to these lists so if you're unsure, please do get in touch.

If you book a training option and it later appears that your dog needs behavioural training, we will suggest a different service.

Behavioural

  • Barking and lunging at people and/or dogs
  • Struggles when left home alone
  • Can't follow training outside if there are dogs, people or cars around
  • Resource guarding
  • Chasing/stalking traffic
  • Barking and/aggressive displays to people coming into or leaving the home
  • Fearfulness/anxiety
  • Reactivity when on walks

Training

  • Poor recall on and off lead
  • Pulling on the lead in excitement (not reacting towards people, dogs, cars, animals)
  • Sit, stay, basic obedience
  • Needing calmer behaviour in the house
  • Jumping up people in excitement
  • Wanting to learn skills like retrieving
